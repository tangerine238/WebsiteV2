Experiences

UW Orbital

On the UW Orbital team, we're building a satallite that will allow amateur radio operators to take real time photos of their current location on Earth. I've personally had the oppurtunity to work on the arducam shield portion of the project, a subsystem of the camera module.

VEX Robotics

VEX Robotics Competition (VRC) is a competitive robotics league where teams compete to build functioning metal robots. The robots are designed to play a variety of games with teams having near complete freedom to design their machine. I was an executive on my school's VEX team. My role was to lead the build and design of the robot. I was also responsible for training newer members of the club, and teaching them the basics of VEX building. As a team, we worked to build a functioning robot capable of driving both autonomously and with user input. The robots were designed and built from scratch using mostly metal parts. In our first year, the team qualified and participated in the Ontario Provincial Competition. We qualified for Worlds in only our second year of competition.

Competitive Trivia

“Reach for the Top” and Consensus Trivia are team-based trivia games, similar to Jeopardy or Quiz Bowl. In teams of four, students attempt to answer a variety of trivia questions ranging from history to sports to current events to historical politics. As the Competitive Trivia club captain at my school, my role was to create a competitive team by recruiting strong players, providing coaching and training to all participants, as well as facilitating practices. I was also responsible for leading the team in tournaments, making strategic decisions on team composition and acting as a liaison with tournament directors.The UTS Competitive Trivia team won the 2023 and 2024 Reach for the Top and Consensus Trivia National Championships.
